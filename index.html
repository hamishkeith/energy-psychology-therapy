What are EFT, NLP and IET?

EFT, or Emotional Freedom Technique

EFT is one of the Energy Psychology Techniques, developed from the ancient Chinese meridian energy system. It is a form of 'emotional acupuncture' – because it involves tapping on the end points of the body's energy meridians, while focusing on emotional issues. It is based on the premise that all negative emotions are the result of a disruption in the body's energy system.

Tapping sends pulses of energy which clear the blockage or disruption, and rebalance the body’s energy system in relation to a specific issue. Shifting energy changes the way the brain processes information about a particular issue, and so tapping while tuned in to the issue is like rewiring or rerouting the brain's conditioned negative response.

In our everyday experience, our bodies carry the effects of our thoughts – positive and negative, limiting and liberating. Our thoughts, beliefs and feelings create the biochemistry of our body and shape our health and wellbeing.

EFT helps release emotional pain; it also works in the same way to release the limiting thoughts and beliefs that get in the way of your success, happiness, health and inner peace.

NLP, or Neuro-linguistic Programming

Neuro – how we process information and store past experiences in our minds

Linguistic – the language we use to communicate our experience

Programming – the patterns or programmes of thought and behaviour that either help or hinder us

NLP uses specific techniques developed from an understanding of how the neurological system functions; and works by using language to change or enhance the patterns (programmes) that control our behaviour.

It is often used by sports and business coaches to identify and enhance ways of achieving personal excellence. People are capable of doing both positive and negative behaviours excellently. When used in the therapeutic context, NLP can be very powerful to help identify and change limiting or harmful behaviours.

IET, or Integrated Energy Techniques

IET brings together the best of energy psychology and ways of working with Ericksonian hypnosis, NLP, coaching, cognitive psychotherapy and psycho-spiritual approaches, to help release emotional pain and open the path to greater success and self-realisation.

Some examples of how I use IET:

  • Clearing negative emotions with EFT before installing positive behaviours with NLP;
  • Using EFT to clear a negative emotion which crops up during NLP work;
  • Clearing difficult memories and changing the way the past is experienced.
